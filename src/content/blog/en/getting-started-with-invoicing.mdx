Getting paid on time is one of the biggest challenges freelancers face. A surprising number of payment delays come down to simple invoicing mistakes — unclear documents, missing details, or no follow-up system. Here are five proven tips to help you invoice like a pro and get paid faster.

## 1. Use a Consistent Numbering System

Every invoice should have a unique, sequential number. This is not just good bookkeeping — it is often a legal requirement. A clear numbering system (like `INV-001`, `INV-002`) makes it easy for both you and your clients to reference specific invoices, and it prevents confusion when disputes arise.

Stick to a format that includes the document type and a sequential counter. If you work across multiple businesses or projects, consider adding a project code prefix. SwiftBill automatically generates and increments invoice numbers for you, so you never have to think about it.

## 2. Set Clear Payment Terms Upfront

Before you start any project, agree on payment terms with your client. Net 15 or Net 30 are common, but do not be afraid to ask for shorter windows — especially for new clients.

Your invoice should prominently display:
- **Due date** (not just "Net 30" — include the actual date)
- **Accepted payment methods**
- **Late payment penalties** (if applicable)
- **Currency** for international clients

Being explicit about expectations reduces the awkward "where's my payment?" conversation later.

## 3. Use Professional Templates

First impressions matter. A well-designed invoice with your logo, consistent branding, and clean layout signals that you run a serious business. Clients are more likely to prioritize payment to someone who presents themselves professionally.

Avoid generic spreadsheet invoices. Instead, use purpose-built templates that include all the fields you need — itemized line items, tax calculations, subtotals, and your business contact information. A polished invoice also reduces questions from your client's accounts payable team, which means faster processing.

## 4. Follow Up Systematically

Do not let unpaid invoices sit indefinitely. Set up a follow-up system:

- **Day 1:** Send the invoice immediately after delivering work
- **3 days before due date:** Friendly reminder email
- **Due date:** Payment reminder with the invoice attached
- **7 days overdue:** Firmer follow-up mentioning late payment terms

Consistency is key. Most late payments are not malicious — clients are simply busy and your invoice slipped through the cracks. A polite, automated reminder system solves this without damaging the relationship.

## 5. Track Everything in One Place

Scattered invoices across email threads, PDFs on your desktop, and mental notes about who owes what is a recipe for lost revenue. Use a single system to create, send, and track all your invoices.

Being able to see at a glance which invoices are paid, pending, or overdue gives you real-time visibility into your cash flow. Pair this with expense tracking and you will have a clear picture of your actual profit — not just your revenue.
